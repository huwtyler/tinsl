Welcome to TINSL

And welcome to the North Pole, we are elves from Santa's Technology, Innovation and New Skills Laboratory. Each Christmas, we invest in people with ideas that could make Christmas better for everyone. We do this by providing tools and kits that encourage wonderful people to invent, innovate and create!

Here are some of the best ideas and inventions from people that TINSL have invested in since it was founded in 1827...

  • 1827

    Pigs in Blankets

    Samuel Higginsbottom

    TINSL'S first investment was in 1827 before technology meant electronics. Sam 'Porky' Higginsbottom's invention revolutionised the Christmas dinner by combining pork sausages with bacon.

  • 1844

    Telephone

    Alexander Graham Bell

    TINSL invested in Bell by providing a small transistor kit when he was a young boy. Years later, the telephone has meant that millions of people can connect and share good wishes on Christmas day with friends and family.

  • 1845

    Christmas Crackers

    Tom Smith

    We kick off Christmas dinner every year by pulling crackers in celebration of this invention!

  • 1882

    Fairy lights

    Edward H. Johnson

    Not many people knew of Johnson as he lay in the shadows of Thomas Edison but we saw his potential for making Christmas more fun!

  • 1886

    The automobile

    Karl Benz

    The best drive of the year is surely driving home for Christmas! Karl Benz knew this so we encouraged his interest for speed with his first tool set including spanners and pliers.

  • 1926

    Television

    John Logie Baird

    Christmas TV wouldn't be possible without our investment in John Logie Baird.

  • 1969

    Barcode

    Sigurd L. Odegard

    Christmas shopping changed in 1969 when it became quicker and easier.

  • 1991

    SatNav

    TINSL

    Originally developed at TINSL's own laboratory, this invention was first named the 'Santa Vehicle System', or SANTAV, it was renamed SATNAV when it hit the consumer shelves the following year.

  • 2016

    It could be you!

People often ask how our elves know who TINSL should invest in. Over the years we've developed our own gadgets to identify those with potential in the field of technology and innovation. Our "BOTS" are just one of these pieces of equipment...

"BOT"

Bauble Observation Telemetry

This year our elves are particularly looking out for 'Internet of Things' enthusiasts.

Many people will find our investments in the form kits, tools and components under their trees or via our network of 'Secret Santas'.

If you're here because you scanned one of our QR codes, congratulations - we wait with anticipation for what you'll create.
